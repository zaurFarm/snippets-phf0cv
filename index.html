The Green Ombré Datejust is the Hardest Rolex to Get Right Now

It is not a Daytona. It is not a GMT-Master. The single hardest Rolex to secure at an authorized dealer in 2026 is a Datejust 36 with the green ombré dial on a Jubilee bracelet. Waitlists have surpassed even the Pepsi GMT at many locations, and the grey market premium tells the story.

The dial that broke the Datejust hierarchy

The green ombré is a sunburst lacquer that transitions from a deep forest green at the perimeter to a lighter sage at the center. Under direct light the gradient shifts dramatically — the center can appear almost silver-green while the edges stay dark. Rolex achieves this with multiple lacquer layers applied at different thicknesses across the dial radius, a technique borrowed from the brand's Stella dial experiments of the 1970s.

Why demand is so extreme

Green has been Rolex's signature color since the brand adopted it for marketing in the 1980s. A green-dial watch from Rolex carries symbolic weight that transcends aesthetics — it is the house color rendered on the wrist. The ombré execution adds rarity because the gradient is harder to produce consistently than a flat-color dial, limiting yields.

Current market pricing

The green ombré Datejust 36 in Oystersteel on Jubilee retails at approximately CHF 9,250. Verified grey market transactions in March and April 2026 ranged from USD 14,800 to USD 16,500 — a premium of sixty to seventy percent. The Rolesor version in Oystersteel and yellow gold commands even higher premiums, with some dealers quoting above USD 20,000.
